#### Supporting Independent Makers Post-Imagine: The Next Step in Growing Your Craft Business

The closure of **Imagine**, a nonprofit marketplace dedicated to supporting independent makers, has left many talented artisans feeling adrift. For those of us who poured our hearts into creating a vibrant community, it wasn’t just a loss of a marketplace; it felt like losing a cherished partner in our creative journeys. But amid this uncertainty lies the opportunity for rebirth—an invitation to redefine how we support one another and how we shape our businesses. So how can we, as independent makers, navigate this new landscape and continue to thrive?

**1. Embrace Community-Focused Solutions**

The core of what we do as independent makers is rooted in community. Imagine thrived because it brought artisans together, fostering genuine relationships and helping them connect with their audience. At **Main Street**, we understand the power of community. By joining a platform that supports local makers, you’re not only gaining access to tools that simplify your business but also to a network of fellow artisans who share your values and goals. Let’s create spaces where we can inspire and uplift each other.

**2. Leverage Simple Tools for Managing Your Business**

As a busy maker, you may find it overwhelming to juggle family responsibilities and your passion. Thankfully, innovative tools now exist to streamline your operations. Main Street’s all-in-one storefront allows you to manage inventory seamlessly across online and in-person sales. You shouldn't have to sacrifice precious family time just to keep track of your orders, and with systems that feel intuitive, you can focus on what truly matters: creating.

**3. Automate Your Marketing Without Losing Your Voice**

In a world that increasingly relies on automation, it’s easy to worry that technology might strip away the personal touch that defines handmade goods. However, automation can serve the artist, not replace them. By utilizing tools that allow you to schedule posts and connect with your audience, you save time and maintain authenticity. Your voice can ring true in your marketing while you devote more energy to your craft.

**4. Stay Transparent About Business Practices**

With larger companies dominating the marketplace, independent artisans must prioritize transparency in their practices. Share your story—what drives you, why you create. Let your customers see the person behind the craft. Building trust lays the foundation for long-lasting relationships with buyers who appreciate not just the product, but the maker behind it. Encourage this sort of transparency in your community, as well. 

**5. Innovate and Adapt Together**

The landscape of handmade goods is continuously changing, and the challenges we face as a community are best met with collaboration. Keep the dialogue open among your peers. Share experiences, and support one another through local events, online forums, and social media groups. Be resourceful. Think about how to package your products differently, or explore wholesale opportunities in local boutiques.

**6. Avoid Overcomplication and Focus on Growth**

Fear of hidden fees or complicated platforms can be daunting. At Main Street, we prioritize simplicity. Our goal is to provide the essential tools you need without overwhelming you. Don’t hesitate to explore options that are intuitive and aligned with your family-centric lifestyle. This moment in time can be your opportunity to grow steadily, allowing for the expansion of your craft in a sustainable way.

Together, we can forge ahead, crafting paths toward not only surviving but thriving as makers. The challenges we face now can lead to greater collaboration and inspiration. Main Street is here to support you—so you can keep making with joy, originality, and humanity.

Join **Main Street** today to connect with a community that values your craft and helps your business thrive!
